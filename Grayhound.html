Traits of the Grayhound

  • Sleeping hours per day: 18 hours
  • Average Size: 2 feet, 1 inch to 2 feet, 6 inches tall at the shoulder
  • Average Weight: 50 to 85 pounds
  • Personality : The Greyhound is a friendly, non-aggressive type of dog, as well as intelligent and independent. Give them a treat though, and they're likely to become a friend for life. They can become shy or timid with mistreatment, even if it's unintentional.
  • Colours: Blue brindle, black & white, fawn brindle, brindle, fawn, white & black, red brindle, black brindle, light red fawn, red & white, light brindle, blue, red fawn, white & brindle tick, dark brindle, black, dark red and white & brindle.
  • Average Life Span: 12 to 15 years.
  • Best Age to Buy: Never ever accept a puppy less than 7 weeks of age, it is vitally important they are with their mother and siblings up to this age. Puppies over twelve weeks should be avoided if possible.
  • Possible Medical Issues: Hip dysplasia, elbow dysplasia, hypothyroidism, and von Willebrand's disease.
  • Pros: Socializing a Greyhound will make it a well-rounded dog. Most Greyhounds have a sweet and well natured. The Greyhounds will adapt nicely to inside living, and a small gardens. These dogs are child-friendly dog, who can tolerate young children who love to give hugs.
  • Cons: Like all dogs, greyhounds should be introduce to other dogs, people, smells, sights and sounds at a young age. Like going on a walk I the park where there will be other dogs and so on. Greyhounds are fairly low energy dogs, but still need a nice daily walk. Greyhounds have a instincts to case pray such as rats, small dogs and small animals, so it is best to have a sturdy fence around the owners garden.
  • Advise It’s best to choose the middle puppy, the one that’s not fighting its siblings or hiding in the corner. ~It is very important to keep the dog on a leash while on a walk, because the greyhound is a fast dog and if its instincts take over is will chase whatever it has seen and it could get lost. ~Greyhounds are large dog that will need a raised feeding dishes for a more comfortable experience. ~Greyhounds can become overweight after retiring from racing, so it is best to watch how much weight they gain and make sure it doesn’t become too much.
